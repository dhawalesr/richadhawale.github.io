Richa Dhawale

Postdoctoral Fellow | Climate Adaptation | Flood & Drought Risk

Richa Dhawale is a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Saskatchewan, working at the intersection of climate change adaptation, flood and drought vulnerability, and the human dimensions of water security. Her research focuses on understanding how overlapping hydro-climatic extremes interact with socio-economic and spatial factors to shape risk, vulnerability, and resilience across river basins.

She holds a PhD in urban and regional planning, with doctoral research centered on developing multidimensional vulnerability frameworks for flood- and drought-prone regions. Her work integrates geospatial analysis, climate indices, and vulnerability assessment to inform adaptation planning and risk-sensitive decision-making.

Her research has been published in international journals and presented at global conferences spanning climate science, water resources, and planning. Her current work examines compound climate risks within large river basins, with an emphasis on policy relevance, equity, and adaptation pathways.
